1. Overview (local-first)

Dabis Content Manager is a desktop app designed to manage and upload videos to YouTube. The App operates locally on your device. When you use YouTube features, the App communicates directly with Google/YouTube Services from your device. We do not run first-party servers for receiving or storing your videos or YouTube account data unless the App explicitly states otherwise.

2. Data we process

2.1 Data you provide locally

  • Videos and media files you choose on your device
  • Video metadata you enter (titles, descriptions, tags, playlists, scheduling settings, etc.)
  • App configuration (preferences, UI settings, local project data)

2.2 Google/YouTube data (via OAuth and the YouTube Data API)

Depending on the permissions ("scopes") you authorize, the App may access and process:

  • Basic account information needed for authentication
  • YouTube channel information associated with the signed-in account
  • Upload-related data and video management actions you initiate (uploads, updates, visibility settings, playlists, etc.)

2.3 Authentication data (tokens)

When you connect your Google account, the App receives OAuth tokens and stores them locally on your device so you don't have to sign in every time.

3. How we use data

We process the data described above only to:

  • Provide the App's core features (prepare, manage, upload, and update videos and metadata on YouTube)
  • Maintain the signed-in session (local token storage)
  • Troubleshoot issues on your device when you choose to share diagnostic information with us (for example, logs you manually export and send)

4. Sharing and transfers

4.1 We do not sell your personal data.

4.2 We do not transfer your Google/YouTube user data to third parties except as necessary to provide the App's user-facing functionality, primarily to Google/YouTube Services when you initiate actions (such as uploading or updating video data).

4.3 Third-party services: Google/YouTube. Your use of Google/YouTube Services is governed by their terms and privacy policies. Data you submit to YouTube is processed by YouTube/Google.

5. Google API Services User Data (Limited Use)

Our use and transfer of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. In particular:

  • We only use Google/YouTube data to provide or improve user-facing features that are prominent in the App's interface.
  • We do not use Google/YouTube data for advertising, retargeting, or data brokering.
  • We do not permit humans to read Google/YouTube data except when necessary for security, legal compliance, or with your explicit instruction (for example, you ask for help and choose to share specific exported logs/files).

6. Legal bases (EEA/UK users)

Where GDPR applies, we process data under:

  • Contract/performance: to provide the App features you request (uploads and management actions you initiate)
  • Consent: for OAuth authorization to access Google/YouTube data (you can withdraw by revoking access)
  • Legitimate interests: basic app reliability and fraud/abuse prevention (only to the extent applicable and proportionate)

7. Storage, retention, and deletion

7.1 Local storage. The App stores your settings, project data, and OAuth tokens locally on your device.

7.2 Retention. Data remains on your device until you delete it (by deleting files, clearing the App's data, or uninstalling the App).

7.3 Revoking Google access. You can revoke the App's access to your Google account at any time via your Google Account's third-party connections/permissions settings. After revocation, the App can no longer access your Google/YouTube data, and YouTube actions will stop working until you reconnect.

8. Security

We use reasonable measures within the App to protect stored tokens and local data, but the security of your device is the most important factor. You are responsible for keeping your operating system, user account, and device storage secure (for example, device encryption and strong login credentials).

9. Your rights

If GDPR (or similar laws) applies, you may have rights such as access, deletion, restriction, objection, and data portability. Because the App is local-first, the fastest way to exercise many of these rights is to delete local app data and/or revoke Google access. You can also contact us at dabinuss@gmail.com for requests related to any data you have actively sent us (for example, support emails).

10. Children

The App is not intended for children. Do not use the App if you are not legally permitted to use Google/YouTube Services or to form a binding contract where you live.

11. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Effective date" at the top indicates when the current version applies.
